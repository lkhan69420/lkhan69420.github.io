Modes of Heat Transfer

Heat is the energy transferred from hotter to colder objects.

Forms of heat transfer:

  • Conduction –– from objects that are physically in contact, especially solids
    • Caused by molecular collisions
    • Conservation of momentum
    • Ironing clothes, touching ice, coffee in mug
    • Occurs when, for example, liquids heat solids, as seen in the last example
  • Convection –– occurs when molecules are not clumped up, such as in fluids, where conduction is negligible
    • Natural convection –– kettle filled with water: heat enters the kettle from the bottom, so the water at the bottom heats up first. Since matter expands when heated, this small pocket of hot water eventually spreads throughout the kettle.
    • Forced convection –– makes use of a fan or pump to force fluid to move (tumble dryer)
  • Radiation –– occurs when objects are not in physical contact with one another; waves
